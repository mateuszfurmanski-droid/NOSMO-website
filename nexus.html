The problem

Construction has many tools. It still lacks one project memory.

Information is split across BIM models, PDFs, schedules, photos, emails, messages, inspection evidence and specialist apps. NEXUS is designed to connect that information without replacing the tools teams already use.

People

Person Cards, roles, companies, responsibilities, permissions and communication context.

Projects

Documents, drawings, tasks, status, issues, timelines, decisions and live project history.

Modules

DoorFlow first as a standalone demo. Future modules can include MEPFlow, SteelFlow, QAFlow, AssetFlow and CommissionFlow.

NEXUS position

Not another isolated app. The intelligent layer between specialist technologies.

NEXUS does not need to duplicate every specialist tool. It provides the AI business memory, project context, document intelligence and workflow layer around them.

BIM / Digital Twin context

BIM gives the model. NEXUS gives the memory around it.

BIM and digital twin environments show what should exist in the building. NEXUS connects that model context with people, tasks, documents, inspections, installation evidence and operational history.

Specialist AR module example

FabStation beside BIM, not instead of BIM.

The clean ecosystem story is: BIM provides model context, FabStation can provide specialist AR / fabrication guidance, and NEXUS connects project memory, workflows, documents, people, QA and handover.

Candidate specialist module

Industrial AR and fabrication-floor intelligence

FabStation is shown here as a specialist AR / fabrication workflow technology that could sit beside BIM and inside the wider NEXUS operating-system trajectory.

NEXUS module roadmap

DoorFlow is the demo wedge. NEXUS is the operating system.

DoorFlow should now operate as a standalone demo application. The main product focus moves to the NEXUS platform: menu, dashboard, Person Cards, companies, projects, documents, business memory, AI assistant, timeline and workspace.

DoorFlow

Door schedules, PDFs, status and handover evidence.

MEPFlow

BIM / MEP coordination, installation context and trade evidence.

SteelFlow

Fabrication, steel workflows and specialist AR integrations.

QAFlow

Quality checks, snags, inspections and proof records.

AssetFlow

Components, equipment, warranties and FM-ready asset records.

CommissionFlow

Testing, commissioning, closeout and verified handover.

Standalone demo

DoorFlow remains the proof-of-work module.

Continue improving DoorFlow, but do not over-spend time perfecting detection now. It should prove the workflow: upload documents, connect schedules, show status, capture evidence and demonstrate the NEXUS module logic.

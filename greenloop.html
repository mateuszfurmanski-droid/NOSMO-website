Automotive plastic recycling / circular material recovery

GreenLoop.

NOSMO GreenLoop focuses on ecological recycling: selected plastic parts from vehicles are identified, separated, cleaned and processed into reusable manufacturing material for pellets, filament and prototype products.

Discuss GreenLoop
From unwanted automotive plastic to usable production material.

The first stage is deliberately practical: controlled feedstock, simple processing, real test batches and visible prototypes.

InputSelected automotive plastic parts from local dismantlers and scrapyards.
ProcessSorting, cleaning, shredding, drying, extrusion and test printing.
OutputReusable plastic material, filament or pellets for prototypes.
Recycling process

Simple, traceable and ecological.

The GreenLoop concept starts with parts that can be identified and controlled. The goal is to avoid mixed, contaminated waste and build a cleaner route from plastic waste to practical manufacturing material.

Feedstock focus

GreenLoop prioritises automotive plastic components that are easier to identify and separate than general mixed waste.

  • Interior and exterior trims
  • Covers and housings
  • Selected bumper material where suitable
  • Low-contamination plastic streams

Ecological value

The project turns local waste into local material and reduces the need for virgin plastic in early prototypes and small production runs.

  • Less landfill pressure
  • Higher-value reuse
  • Local circular supply chain
  • Visible recycling evidence

Prototype route

Early prototypes prove material usability before larger manufacturing claims are made.

  • Workshop tooling
  • Construction accessories
  • Protective components
  • Test prints and material samples
Current focus
Recycling first.

The current GreenLoop page should communicate practical recycling and material recovery. Carbon fibre and robotic composite manufacturing remain future development routes, not the core first claim.

Business plan fit
From waste to material.

GreenLoop can be positioned as a small circular manufacturing lab: sourcing automotive plastic waste, processing it into reusable material and validating it through visible prototype products.
